TOP SECRET

The envelope arrived without a stamp, slipped beneath Tunde’s apartment door sometime after midnight. His name was written in careful block letters, the kind used by people who didn’t want their handwriting recognized. Inside was a single photograph—grainy, black-and-white—showing him from across the street, taken just two evenings before. On the back, a message: “You kept the secret. So have I.” Tunde felt the room shrink around him.

He tried to convince himself it was a prank, but the second envelope arrived the next day, this time on his office desk. No one saw who delivered it. Inside was a photocopy of an old newspaper clipping about a warehouse fire—an accident, the report had said.

The third message came as a handwritten note slid into his car’s windshield wiper: “Meet me where it happened. Midnight.” When he arrived at the abandoned warehouse, the air was still heavy with the scent of rust and rain
